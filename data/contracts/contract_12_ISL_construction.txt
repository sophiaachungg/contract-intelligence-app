BRANCH: ISL (Integrated Services & Logistics)
PARTIES: Ergon Integrated Services & Logistics, LLC ("Owner") | Delta Industrial Contractors, LLC ("Contractor")
EFFECTIVE DATE: August 1, 2024
EXPIRATION DATE: July 31, 2026
AUTO-RENEWAL: No

RECITALS
Owner requires industrial construction, maintenance, and specialty construction services at its terminal and pipeline facilities. Contractor is engaged in industrial pipeline construction, tank maintenance, and facility construction services.

1. SCOPE
Contractor shall perform construction and maintenance services as requested by Owner through individual Work Orders issued under this Master Services Agreement. Each Work Order shall specify scope, schedule, and compensation.

2. COMPENSATION
Work shall be compensated on a time-and-materials basis per the Labor Rate Schedule in Exhibit A unless a fixed-price Work Order is mutually agreed. Owner shall pay undisputed invoices within 30 days. Contractor may suspend work after 45 days of non-payment.

3. SAFETY
Contractor shall comply with Owner's Site Safety Plan and all applicable OSHA regulations. Contractor shall conduct a Job Hazard Analysis ("JHA") prior to beginning any non-routine work. All Contractor personnel working at Owner's facilities shall complete Owner's site safety orientation.

**RISK FLAG - SAFETY COMPLIANCE ENFORCEMENT:** The agreement requires Contractor to comply with Owner's Safety Plan but does not specify Owner's right to audit Contractor's safety practices or remove personnel for safety violations without triggering a contract dispute. This limits Owner's ability to enforce safety standards in real time.

4. INDEMNIFICATION
Contractor shall indemnify Owner against claims for bodily injury or property damage arising from Contractor's work, including Contractor's subcontractors.

**RISK FLAG - INDEMNIFICATION SCOPE:** The indemnification clause is written as a "broad form" indemnity that may not be enforceable in Mississippi, which limits anti-indemnity provisions under certain circumstances. The clause should be reviewed for compliance with Mississippi's Oilfield Anti-Indemnity Statute if any work involves oil and gas facilities.

5. INSURANCE
Contractor shall maintain: (a) workers' compensation per statutory limits; (b) commercial general liability $2,000,000 per occurrence, $5,000,000 aggregate; (c) auto liability $1,000,000 CSL; (d) umbrella/excess $5,000,000.

6. LIENS
Contractor shall provide lien waivers with each invoice. Owner may withhold final payment until Contractor delivers final unconditional lien waivers.

7. SUBCONTRACTORS
Contractor may use subcontractors with Owner's prior written approval. Contractor remains fully responsible for all subcontractor work.

8. WARRANTY
Contractor warrants all work for one year from completion. Defective work shall be remediated at Contractor's expense within 30 days of written notice.

GOVERNING LAW: State of Mississippi
